Secret to Washing White Shirts to Make Them Shine Like New

June 29, 2024

White shirts are an indispensable item in everyone's wardrobe, from students to working people. However, washing clothes to keep white shirts bright as new is not always easy. Stains, sweat and improper washing methods can make white shirts turn yellow and lose their freshness. Below are tips to wash white shirts bright as new from HC Vietnam to help you solve this problem.

1. Sort Clothes Before Washing

Classification By Color:

How to wash white and colored clothes to avoid dye transfer from colored clothes to white clothes. This helps white clothes retain their brightness and prevent fading.

Classification by Material:

Wash delicate fabrics such as silk, linen and thick fabrics such as denim, cotton separately to avoid damaging the fabric fibers by washing them together with different fabrics.

2. Use the Right Detergent and Bleach

The right detergent and bleach will effectively clean white clothes without damaging the fabric.

Specialized Laundry Detergent:

Use a detergent specifically designed for white clothes to keep colors fresh and remove stains effectively.

Bleach:

Use oxygen bleach or baking soda to brighten white shirts without damaging the fabric. Avoid using chlorine bleach, which can damage fabric fibers and cause yellowing.

3. How to Wash Clothes Properly

Washing clothes properly not only helps clean them but also protects the fabric fibers and keeps white shirts bright and new.

Washing Temperature:

Wash white shirts at 30-40 degrees Celsius. This temperature helps remove stains and bacteria without damaging the fabric fibers.

Wash Cycle:

Choose a gentle or medium wash cycle to avoid stretching and damaging the fabric. If your white shirt has stubborn stains, soak it in detergent mixed with a little baking soda or vinegar before washing.

Using Fabric Softener:

Fabric softener not only softens clothes, it also reduces static and keeps clothes wrinkle-free.

4. How to Iron a Shirt Properly

The correct way to iron a shirt helps keep it flat and in shape.

Prepare:

Use a high-quality iron that can adjust the temperature to suit each type of fabric. Before ironing, dampen the shirt with a spray bottle of water or iron while the shirt is still slightly damp.

How to Iron:

Start at the collar, then the shoulders, the body, and finally the sleeves. Iron from the inside out to avoid shiny surfaces. Use steam to soften the fabric and make ironing easier.

5. Drying and Preserving White Shirts

Drying and storing white shirts properly is the step to keep them bright and new.

Drying Clothes:

Dry white shirts in a cool place, away from direct sunlight to avoid damaging the fabric. White shirts should be hung horizontally on hangers to keep their shape and avoid wrinkling.

Preserve:

Store white shirts in a cool, dry place. Avoid exposing white shirts to items that may cause yellowing, such as perfume, cosmetics, or other chemicals.

6. Handling Stubborn Stains

Treating new stains will help keep your white shirts clean and free of yellowing.

Sweat Stains:

Use vinegar or lemon to remove sweat stains from white shirts. Apply vinegar or lemon juice to the stain, leave for a few minutes, then wash as usual.

Food Stains:

Baking soda is an effective natural cleaner for food stains. Mix baking soda with water to form a paste, apply to the stain and let sit for 15-20 minutes before washing.

Ink Stains:

Use alcohol or an alcohol-based cleaner to remove ink stains. Apply alcohol to the stain, rub gently, and then wash with detergent.

7. Notes When Washing Clothes

Some small but important notes when washing clothes to protect your white shirts.

Using Laundry Bag:

Use a laundry bag to protect delicate and delicate fabrics such as silk and linen when washing in the washing machine. The bag helps reduce friction and stretching of the fabric.

Hand Washing For Delicate Whites:

For sensitive and expensive white shirts, hand wash to protect the fabric and keep the shirt looking new.
